Projects

Semi-Supervised Hate Speech Detection

Some research has been done to detect hate speech and offensive language in tweets. Most of these approaches look at the modern methods for text classification and apply them to hate speech. I built off their work by supplementing their data with unlabeled data to improve the model's power. By building embeddings over the combined corpora and also adding the unseen tweets during training, I was able to improve their results.

Definition Modeling

In Definition Modeling, Noraset et al. demostrate the effectiveness of using word embeddings to extract the word's definition. The researchers used a RNN language model, as well as a character-level CNN.

My goal task is to improve the overall system. By adding the part-of-speech to the model, this turns the issue of a one-to-many word to definition closer to a one-to-one word to definition problem.

Editing via Machine Translation

In Google's Neural Machine Translation System, Wu et al. propose a sequence-to-sequence LSTM encoder-decoder model to translate between languages.

I helped modify the system to edit English sentences in resumes. By looking at unedited resumes as the source language, and edited resumes as the target language, we were able to use Google's model to our advantage. We also implemented a few other features such as transfer learning and backtranslation to improve the model and combat the lack of high quality data that we had.

Crowd Density Monitoring

I built a sensor system that tracks the number of people going in and out of a space, such as university cafeteria, library, etc. Using wireless Arduinos as the sensor system, live data is sent to a Firebase backend. A frontend website geared towards enterprise use gives users an overview of the entire system.

Using regression and machine learning, I tried to apply predictive capabilities to the system, so that administrators and students on campus can see how crowded facilities around campus are at a given time and in the future.

The Modern NBA Positions

In the current modern era of NBA basketball, research has been done to reclassify player positions. The 5 standard positions in the NBA oversimplify the vast range of skillsets players have. A few different methods of have been used to reclassify positions.

My goal was to go beyond just reclassifying players. I looked at a few different metrics, such as regularized adjusted plus-minus, win shares, efficiency, and on/off splits. By looking at stats of player types while they are on the floor with other combinations of player types, many insights can be drawn of what type of player combinations work well together.
